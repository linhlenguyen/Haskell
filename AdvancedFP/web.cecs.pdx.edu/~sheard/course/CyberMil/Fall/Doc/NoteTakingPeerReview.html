Note Taking Peer Review

Name of the author of the notes being reviewed ________________________________________

Name of the note taking coach giving feedback _________________________________________

  • List the 3 best things about the notes. Be specific, tell why you found these things useful.












  • In your opinion, what items constitute the weakest part of the notes. Be specific, and suggest 2 ways that the author could strengthen these items.








  • Are the notes elaborated to sufficient depth? Point out 2 important ideas you recall from your reading of the paper that could be given more detail in the notes you are reading.








  • Do the ideas in the notes flow together to make a unifiable whole? Could you tell from reading the notes, what the paper the notes summarize was about? In 3 or 4 sentences, reiterate in your own words, what you think the paper is about from reading the notes you are reviewing. (I ask the note taking coach to do this summary, so that the author can judge if he has expressed his intent clearly. If your idea of the paper and the author's (own) idea of the paper differ widely, this tells the note's author something.)
