    2. Effectiveness of Internet vs. Face to Face Support Groups

      • 56,000,000 Americans go online for help with health problems
      • Many are minorities or socio-economically deprived.
      • No understanding of the effectiveness of face-to-face v.s. online support groups.
      • Face-to-face support groups are 5 times more costly to build and support than on-line support groups.
      • On-line are even more cost effective in under-served rural areas.
      • In the area of woment with breast cancer, it is easier to recruit and retain woment into on-line support groups.
      • Participants showed significant decreases in depression.
      • Expression of negative emotions (fear, anxiety) led to greater eventual improvement.
      • Leader led support groups had bigger gains than ones not led by trained professionals.

    3. Faking Pain and Suffering In Internet Support Group

      • People fake disease symptons to elicit sympathy from "fellow sufferers"
      • People who are living through genuine crisies and tradgedies, nevertheless extend themselves to comfort others.
      • Fakers are often exposed by their own lies and inconsistencies.
      • The internet lends itself to deception.
      • The internet has vast amounts of information for those want to pretend they are sick.
      • Such disceptions are not new to the internet. Various forms (Munchausen.s Syndrome) have been know for over 100 years.
      • Deception seems to be a way of attracting attention, nurturing, sympathy and care.
      • Being treated as a "hero" for courage in the face of adversity.

    4. Criteria for evaluation of Internet Information Resources

      • Developed by libraians who have years of experience evaluating what resources are valuable.
      • Lost if information. Where do you start? Search engines, online medical libraies.
      • How can tell if its any good?
        1. Who sponsors the page. Govt. Educational institution, professional, commercial
        2. How current is it?
        3. Is it factual or opinion?
        4. Who is the audience. Consumer or Health professional?
      • Tecno-speak often limits immediate useful ness.
      • Internet Foundation Code of Conduct rules: Authoritative , Complementarity, Privacy, Attribution, Justifiability, Transparency, Financial disclosure, Advertising policy.
